Already out of school and into a career? You possess knowledge, skills, and perspectives that students cannot obtain from books. Whether you have been working for two years twenty, we want to learn from your experience. Come visit us or let us visit you. Let's enjoy a conversation over a meal.

What's in it for you? Lunch or dinner is on us. You become a professor for an hour without having to prepare any lectures or grade any examinations. You are the authority. We write down what you say. We give you the part of our job that is most fun—engagement with bright and curious young people.

Now enrolled in college? You will not learn all that you want to know in classrooms. Let us introduce you to people who, starting at places similar to places you now occupy, went on to build satisfying careers and lives. We will make the conversations easy. See the variety of opportunities others have found. Discover paths that will take you to where you want to be.

The choice of a major course of study is not the same as the choice of a career. Conversations with people in fields far removed from your own could reveal unexpected ways of using your special talents to make a nice life for yourself and a better world for others.

FAQ

Where have students gone in the past?
Students have visited a company that makes instruments for aircraft, a company that makes ruggedized computers, a large hospital (where they viewed equipment for the generation of power in emergencies), and an historical society (where they saw a restored low head hydroelectric dam).
With whom have students conversed?
Students have met a marketing manager for a major league team, reporters and editorialists who contribute to the nation's most prominent newspapers, a physician who is also a consulting engineer and an editor of a leading scientific journal.
